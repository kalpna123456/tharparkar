Gori Temple

Gori Temple, also known as Gori Mandir or Gori jo Mandar, is an ancient Jain temple located in the Tharparkar district of Sindh, Pakistan. It was built around the 14th century, approximately in 1375 AD, and is dedicated to Lord Parshvanath, the 23rd Tirthankara of Jainism. Gori Temple is considered one of the oldest and most beautiful Jain temples in Sindh.

Architecture

The architecture of Gori Temple is remarkable and artistically rich. Religious Importance

Gori Temple holds deep religious significance for the Jain community. It was once an active place of worship where devotees came for prayer and meditation. The temple enshrined the idol of Lord Parshvanath, who is highly revered in Jainism. Even though worship no longer takes place here, it remains an important spiritual and historical site for followers of the Jain faith.

The Story of Umar and Marvi

The story of Umar and Marvi is one of the most famous folktales of Tharparkar, symbolizing loyalty, purity, and love for one‚Äôs homeland. Marvi, a noble village girl from Malir, was admired for her beauty, kindness, and strong character. King Umar, captivated by her grace, brought her to his fort in Umarkot and asked her to marry him. But Marvi refused, choosing her people and her desert homeland over a life of luxury. Despite being kept in the fort for months, her heart remained loyal to her village. Impressed by her honesty and unwavering courage, Umar eventually set her free. Today, Marvi is celebrated as a heroine of integrity, and her story remains deeply rooted in the cultural heritage of Sindh.

Karoonhjar Mountains

Karoonjhar Mountain ‚Äì The Beauty of Tharparkar

Karoonjhar Mountain is one of the most famous and beautiful natural landmarks of Tharparkar, located near Nagarparkar. These ancient granite hills are millions of years old, and their beauty becomes even more striking during sunrise and sunset. In the monsoon season, small streams flow through the rocks, creating lush green scenery all around. Karoonjhar is not only a scenic place but also an important part of local history, culture, and folklore. It is a wonderful destination for photographers, nature lovers, and travelers visiting Tharparkar.

Ghadi Bhit

Ghadi Bhit is a prominent sand-hill in the town of Mithi, Tharparkar, serving as the highest vantage point in the area. Perched atop a dune, it offers visitors a stunning panoramic view of Mithi, especially at sunset when the town lights sparkle and the sky glows in warm hues. Historically, it was the site of a small fort or check post built by the Talpur rulers to guard against invaders.
